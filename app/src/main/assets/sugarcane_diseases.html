5.DISEASES OF SUGARCANE
There are several diseases that can affect sugarcane crops, some of which
are:
1.Red rot :- This fungal disease causes reddish discoloration of the
lower stems of sugarcane plants, which eventually leads to wilting and
death of
the plants.
=>Red rot disease in sugarcane crops typically occurs during the monsoon
season when there is high humidity and rainfall. The disease is caused by
the fungus Colletotrichum falcatum, which can infect the sugarcane plants
through wounds or natural openings such as leaf scars. The fungus can
survive in the soil and plant debris for several years and can spread
through infected plant material, irrigation water, and machinery. Early
symptoms of red rot disease include reddish discoloration of the lower
stem and the appearance of small, dark spots on the leaves. As the
disease progresses, the infected plants can wilt and die, leading to
significant losses in crop yield. Control measures for red rot disease
include the use of resistant varieties, crop rotation, and application of
fungicides.
2.Smut :- This fungal disease causes black, sooty growths on the flower
spikes of sugarcane plants, which can reduce crop yields.
=>Smut disease in sugarcane crops typically occurs during warm and humid
weather conditions. The disease is caused by the fungus Sporisorium
scitamineum, which can infect the sugarcane plants through the flowers.
The fungus can survive in the soil and plant debris for several years and
can spread through infected plant material and machinery. Early symptoms
of smut disease include the appearance of black, sooty growths on the
flower spikes of the sugarcane plants. As the disease progresses, the
infected plants can produce fewer and smaller stalks, leading to
significant losses in crop yield. Control measures for smut disease
include the use of resistant varieties, destruction of infected plant
material, and application of fungicides. Crop rotation can also help to
reduce the incidence of the disease by preventing the buildup of the
fungus in the soil.
3.Leaf scald :- This bacterial disease causes yellowing and necrosis of
the leaves of sugarcane plants, which can eventually lead to the death of
the
plant.
=>Leaf scald disease in sugarcane crops typically occurs during periods
of high humidity and rainfall. The disease is caused by the bacterium
Xanthomonas albilineans, which can infect the sugarcane plants through
wounds or natural openings such as leaf scars. The bacterium can survive
in the plant debris and can spread through infected plant material,
irrigation water, and machinery. Early symptoms of leaf scald disease
include yellowing and necrosis of the leaves of the sugarcane plants,
which can progress to the upper leaves and cause the death of the plant.
Control measures for leaf scald disease include the use of resistant
varieties, removal and destruction of infected plant material, and
application of antibiotics or copper-based fungicides. Good crop
management practices such as proper sanitation, crop rotation, and
avoiding the use of infected planting material can also help to reduce
the incidence of the disease.
4.Ratoon stunting disease - This viral disease can stunt the growth of
sugarcane plants and reduce crop yields.
=>Ratoon stunting disease in sugarcane crops can occur at any time during
the growing season. The disease is caused by a group of closely related
viruses, including Sugarcane yellow leaf virus (SCYLV), which can infect
the sugarcane plants through insect vectors such as aphids. The viruses
can also spread through infected planting material, sap, and machinery.
Early symptoms of ratoon stunting disease include stunted growth,
yellowing and reddening of the leaves, and reduction in the number and
quality of the stalks. The infected plants may also produce smaller and
more fibrous stalks, which can reduce the crop yield. Control measures
for ratoon stunting disease include the use of resistant varieties,
removal and destruction of infected plant material, and insect control
measures to prevent the spread of the virus. Good crop management
practices such as crop rotation, avoiding the use of infected planting
material, and maintaining a healthy and vigorous crop can also help to
reduce the incidence of the disease.
5.Yellow leaf virus :- This viral disease can cause yellowing and
necrosis of the leaves of sugarcane plants, which can reduce crop yields.
=>Yellow leaf virus disease in sugarcane crops typically occurs during
periods of high temperature and humidity. The disease is caused by the
Sugarcane yellow leaf virus (SCYLV), which can infect the sugarcane
plants through insect vectors such as aphids. The virus can also spread
through infected planting material, sap, and machinery. Early symptoms of
yellow leaf virus disease include yellowing and necrosis of the leaves of
the sugarcane plants, which can progress to the upper leaves and cause
the death of the plant. The infected plants may also produce fewer and
smaller stalks, which can reduce the crop yield. Control measures for
yellow leaf virus disease include the use of resistant varieties, removal
and destruction of infected plant material, and insect control measures
to prevent the spread of the virus. Good crop management practices such
as crop rotation, avoiding the use of infected planting material, and
maintaining a healthy and vigorous crop can also help to reduce the
incidence of the disease.
6.Sugarcane mosaic virus :- This viral disease can cause mosaic-like
patterns on the leaves of sugarcane plants, which can reduce crop yields.
=>Sugarcane mosaic virus (SCMV) disease in sugarcane crops can occur at
any time during the growing season. The disease is caused by the
Sugarcane mosaic virus, which can infect the sugarcane plants through
several means including mechanical transmission through cutting tools,
infected planting materials, and insect vectors such as aphids. Early
symptoms of SCMV include a mosaic pattern of yellow and green on the
leaves of the plant, followed by stunted growth and reduced yield.
Infected plants may also exhibit systemic chlorosis and produce malformed
stalks. Control measures for SCMV disease include the use of certified
disease-free planting materials, the removal and destruction of infected
plant material, and the use of insecticides to control aphid vectors.
Good crop management practices, such as regular field scouting and the
use of resistant cultivars, can also help to reduce the incidence of the
disease.
7.Pokkah boeng disease :- This fungal disease can cause yellowing and
wilting of the leaves of sugarcane plants, which can reduce crop yields.
=>Pokkah boeng disease in sugarcane crops typically occurs during periods
of high rainfall and temperature. The disease is caused by the Sugarcane
bacilliform virus (SCBV), which can infect the sugarcane plants through
several means including infected planting material, sap, and insect
vectors such as planthoppers. Early symptoms of Pokkah boeng disease
include dark green streaks or bands on the leaves of the plant, which may
appear on the whole leaf or part of it. Infected plants may also exhibit
stunted growth and produce malformed stalks, which can reduce the crop
yield. Control measures for Pokkah boeng disease include the use of
certified disease-free planting materials, the removal and destruction of
infected plant material, and the use of insecticides to control
planthopper vectors. Good crop management practices, such as regular
field scouting and the use of resistant cultivars, can also help to
reduce the incidence of the disease.
