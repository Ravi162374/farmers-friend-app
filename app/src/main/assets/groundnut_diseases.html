7.DISEASES OF
GROUNDNUT
Groundnut crops are susceptible to a range of diseases that can affect
their growth, development, and yield. Some common diseases that affect
groundnut crops include:
1.Early leaf spot :- This fungal disease causes the leaves to develop
small, circular lesions with a reddish-brown center and a yellow halo.
The
disease can progress rapidly, causing defoliation and reduced yield.
=>Early leaf spot (also known as Cercospora leaf spot) is a fungal
disease that can occur in groundnut crops. The disease is caused by the
fungus Cercosporidium personatum and is characterized by the development
of small, circular lesions with a reddish-brown center and a yellow halo
on the leaves. Early leaf spot can occur at any stage of crop growth, but
is most commonly observed during the vegetative and early reproductive
stages.
The disease typically starts on the lower leaves of the plant and can
spread to the upper leaves if favorable conditions persist. The lesions
can coalesce, leading to defoliation and reduced photosynthesis, which
can result in yield loss. Early leaf spot thrives in warm, humid
conditions and can be favored by prolonged leaf wetness and high
humidity.
Control measures for early leaf spot in groundnut crops include the use
of disease-resistant cultivars, crop rotation, good cultural practices
such as sanitation (removal and destruction of infected plant debris),
and weed management to reduce the spread of the disease. Fungicides can
also be used as a preventive or curative measure, depending on the
severity of the disease and local regulations. Early detection and
monitoring of disease symptoms, as well as timely application of control
measures, are important to manage early leaf spot and minimize its impact
on groundnut crops. Consultation with local agricultural extension
services or experts can provide specific recommendations for managing
early leaf spot in groundnut crops in a particular region.
2.Late leaf spot :- This fungal disease is similar to early leaf spot,
but the lesions are larger and more irregular in shape. The disease can
cause
significant defoliation and yield loss.
=>Late leaf spot is a fungal disease that can occur in groundnut crops
caused by the fungus Phaeoisariopsis personata. The disease is
characterized by the development of large, irregularly shaped lesions
with a dark brown center and a yellow halo on the leaves. Late leaf spot
usually appears after early leaf spot, typically during the reproductive
stage of the crop.
The disease can cause significant defoliation, leading to reduced
photosynthesis and yield loss. Late leaf spot can also increase the
susceptibility of the crop to other diseases and pests, such as stem rot
and pod rot. The disease is favored by warm, humid conditions and can
spread rapidly in wet weather.
Control measures for late leaf spot in groundnut crops include the use of
disease-resistant cultivars, crop rotation, good cultural practices such
as sanitation (removal and destruction of infected plant debris), and
weed management to reduce the spread of the disease. Fungicides can also
be used as a preventive or curative measure, depending on the severity of
the disease and local regulations. Early detection and monitoring of
disease symptoms, as well as timely application of control measures, are
important to manage late leaf spot and minimize its impact on groundnut
crops. Consultation with local agricultural extension services or experts
can provide specific recommendations for managing late leaf spot in
groundnut crops in a particular region.
3.Stem rot :- This fungal disease affects the stems and pods of the
plant, causing them to rot and become brittle. The disease can be
particularly
severe in wet conditions and can cause significant yield loss.
=>Stem rot is a fungal disease that can occur in groundnut crops. The
disease is caused by the fungus Sclerotium rolfsii and is characterized
by the development of watery lesions on the stem, which can quickly
expand and cause the plant to wilt and die. The fungus can produce hard,
black sclerotia that can survive in the soil and infect subsequent crops.
Stem rot is favored by warm, humid conditions and can be spread through
infected plant debris, soil, or water. The disease can cause significant
yield loss, especially if it occurs early in the crop growth cycle.
Control measures for stem rot in groundnut crops include the use of
disease-resistant cultivars, crop rotation, good cultural practices such
as sanitation (removal and destruction of infected plant debris), and
weed management to reduce the spread of the disease. Fungicides can also
be used as a preventive or curative measure, depending on the severity of
the disease and local regulations. Early detection and monitoring of
disease symptoms, as well as timely application of control measures, are
important to manage stem rot and minimize its impact on groundnut crops.
Consultation with local agricultural extension services or experts can
provide specific recommendations for managing stem rot in groundnut crops
in a particular region.
4.Groundnut rosette disease :- This viral disease is transmitted by the
groundnut aphid and causes the leaves to become yellow and stunted, with
the characteristic rosette-like appearance. The disease can cause
significant yield loss and is particularly severe in sub-Saharan Africa.
=>Groundnut rosette disease is a viral disease that can occur in
groundnut crops. The disease is caused by a complex of viruses, including
Groundnut rosette assistor virus (GRAV), Groundnut rosette umbravirus
(GRV), and Groundnut rosette virus (GRV). The disease is characterized by
the development of rosette-like symptoms on infected plants, which
includes yellowing of leaves, stunting, and the development of excessive
branching. Infected plants may also have a bushy appearance and produce
few or no pods.
The disease is transmitted by the groundnut aphid (Aphis craccivora) and
the disease can spread rapidly in areas with high aphid populations. The
disease can cause significant yield loss, with total crop failure
reported in severe cases.
Control measures for groundnut rosette disease in groundnut crops include
the use of disease-resistant cultivars, insect control measures to manage
aphid populations, and the use of cultural practices such as sanitation
(removal and destruction of infected plants and debris) to reduce the
spread of the disease. Consultation with local agricultural extension
services or experts can provide specific recommendations for managing
groundnut rosette disease in groundnut crops in a particular region.
5.Cercospora leaf spot :- This fungal disease causes small, circular
lesions with a grayish-white center and a brown margin on the leaves. The
disease can cause defoliation and yield loss, particularly in wet
conditions.
=>Cercospora leaf spot is a fungal disease that can occur in groundnut
crops. The disease is caused by the fungus Cercospora arachidicola and is
characterized by the development of small, dark spots on the leaves,
which can expand and coalesce to form large lesions. Infected leaves may
turn yellow and drop prematurely, leading to defoliation and reduced
photosynthesis. The disease can also affect pods, causing discoloration
and reduced quality.
Cercospora leaf spot is favored by warm and humid conditions, and can be
spread through infected plant debris and wind-blown spores. The disease
can cause significant yield loss, especially in areas with high disease
pressure.
Control measures for Cercospora leaf spot in groundnut crops include the
use of disease-resistant cultivars, good cultural practices such as crop
rotation, sanitation (removal and destruction of infected plant debris),
and weed management to reduce the spread of the disease. Fungicides can
also be used as a preventive or curative measure, depending on the
severity of the disease and local regulations. Early detection and
monitoring of disease symptoms, as well as timely application of control
measures, are important to manage Cercospora leaf spot and minimize its
impact on groundnut crops. Consultation with local agricultural extension
services or experts can provide specific recommendations for managing
Cercospora leaf spot in groundnut crops in a particular region.
6.Peanut smut :- This fungal disease affects the pods of the plant,
causing them to become distorted and covered in a black, powdery spore
mass.
The disease can cause significant yield loss and can be particularly
severe in wet conditions.
=>Peanut smut is a fungal disease that can occur in groundnut crops. The
disease is caused by the fungus Thecaphora frezii and is characterized by
the development of galls or black, powdery masses (sori) on different
parts of the plant, including leaves, stems, and pods. Infected plants
may also show stunted growth and reduced yield. The disease can spread
through infected plant debris and soil.
Peanut smut is favored by warm and humid conditions and can cause
significant yield loss, especially in areas with high disease pressure.
The disease can be managed through a combination of cultural practices,
chemical control, and the use of disease-resistant cultivars.
Control measures for peanut smut in groundnut crops include good cultural
practices such as crop rotation, sanitation (removal and destruction of
infected plant debris), and weed management to reduce the spread of the
disease. Fungicides can also be used as a preventive or curative measure,
depending on the severity of the disease and local regulations. The use
of disease-resistant cultivars is an effective and sustainable way to
manage peanut smut in groundnut crops. Early detection and monitoring of
disease symptoms, as well as timely application of control measures, are
important to manage peanut smut and minimize its impact on groundnut
crops. Consultation with local agricultural extension services or experts
can provide specific recommendations for managing peanut smut in
groundnut crops in a particular region.
Control measures for these diseases include the use of disease-resistant
cultivars, crop rotation, good cultural practices such as sanitation and
weed management, and the use of fungicides and insecticides as needed.
Early detection and monitoring of disease symptoms are also important to
